FAQ

What is the Mobile Native Foundation (MNF)?

The MNF is a forum dedicated to improving engineering for large-scale mobile applications.

Why is it needed?

There lacks a neutral and openly governed forum for companies to collaborate on ideas and software to improve the state of large scale mobile application development.

What will it do and / or what will the output of its work?

The MNF provides a place to collaborate on open source projects and discuss wide ranging topics in order to improve processes and technologies for large-scale Android and iOS applications.

Who is initially involved?

Organizations contributing to this effort include Airbnb, Corellium, Elotl, Flare.build, GitHub, GogoApps, Haystack, Line, LinkedIn, Lyft, Microsoft, Robinhood, screenplay.dev, Slack, Solid Software, Spotify and Uber.

How does this relate to CNCF?

The Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF) is a sister organization of MNF under the Linux Foundation which hosts a variety of open source collaboration efforts. The CNCF is focused on cloud native infrastructure and the MNF is focused on mobile applications, they have different scopes even though they share the same parent organization under the LF.

How do I contribute a project to the MNF?

It's early days as we are still forming, start a discussion on GitHub to gauge community interest and get involved with the formation of the new foundation.
